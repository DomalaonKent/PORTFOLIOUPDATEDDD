VetConnect

Figma Mobile App UI/UX Telehealth

A telehealth mobile app connecting pet owners with licensed veterinarians for real-time video consultations, AI-assisted triage, and digital prescriptions — designed to make veterinary care accessible anytime, anywhere.

Gallery

Overview

The VetConnect Mobile App project is an application designed to connect pet owners and veterinarians. It aims to make access to veterinary services and pet health management easier and more convenient.

  • Project Title: VetConnect Mobile App
  • The Challenge: Pet owners often struggle to find the right veterinarian, schedule appointments, and track their pet's health. A solution was needed to speed up and simplify this process.
  • My Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Project Duration: Approximately 2–3 months
  • Tools Used: Figma

Research & Discovery

Research was conducted to understand the needs of pet owners and veterinarians, as well as the existing problems in accessing veterinary services.

  • User Research: Conducted interviews and surveys with pet owners and veterinarians to understand their pain points, needs, and expectations from a mobile application for veterinary services.
  • User Personas: Developed personas representing different types of users — such as "Busy Pet Owner" and "Clinic Veterinarian" — to guide the design and ensure the varied needs of both sides were addressed.
  • Competitive Analysis: Reviewed existing mobile apps or platforms for veterinary services to identify best practices, market gaps, and opportunities for differentiation for VetConnect.

Ideation & Strategy

Based on research findings, the strategy and information architecture were outlined to create a user-friendly and efficient mobile app — including planning user flows and building initial layouts.

  • User Journey Maps: Mapped out user journeys for key tasks such as finding a veterinarian, booking an appointment, and accessing pet medical records — to ensure a smooth and intuitive experience.
  • Information Architecture (IA): Outlined the app's information structure, including veterinarian profiles, service listings, appointment calendar, and medical records.
  • Wireframes (Low-Fidelity): Created low-fidelity wireframes for the app's key screens to quickly visualize layout and information flow, enabling early feedback on layout and functionality.

Design & Execution

Ideas and strategy were translated into actual design, focused on creating a clear, efficient, and visually appealing interface for the mobile app with a consistent visual identity.

  • Design System: Built a design system with a clean color palette (blue and green for health and trust), readable typography, and easy-to-understand icons. Ensured consistency throughout the app.
  • High-Fidelity Designs: Designed the final screens in Figma with complete visual design — including detailed UI elements, imagery, and interactive components — showcasing the overall look and feel of the app.
  • Prototypes: Created a clickable Figma prototype to simulate the interactive user experience and present the system flow to stakeholders and for usability testing.

Testing & Iteration

To ensure the effectiveness of the design, testing and user feedback were conducted to continuously improve the system and address user needs.

  • Usability Testing: Conducted usability testing with target users (pet owners and veterinarians) to identify any issues with usage, navigation, and feature comprehension — and to gather feedback on the overall experience.
  • Feedback & Changes: Based on feedback from usability testing, design changes were made to improve usability, accessibility, and the overall user experience — resulting in a more refined and effective app.

Results & Reflection

The VetConnect Mobile App aims to make veterinary services easier and more accessible — improving the health and wellbeing of pets and providing peace of mind to their owners.

  • Impact / Metrics: The designed mobile app provides a centralized platform for finding veterinarians, booking appointments, and managing pet medical records — expected to increase user engagement and improve access to veterinary services.
  • Key Takeaways: The project emphasized the importance of understanding the distinct needs of two primary user groups (pet owners and veterinarians) and building a solution that provides value to both sides.
  • Next Steps: Potential addition of features such as telehealth consultations, an in-app pharmacy, or integration with pet insurance providers.
