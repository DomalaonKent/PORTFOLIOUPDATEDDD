Overview

The Damayan Community App is a mobile application concept designed to connect Filipinos in times of need. It aims to build resilience in local communities through technology — allowing neighbors to ask for and offer help.

  • Project Title: Damayan Community App
  • The Challenge: Despite the cultural spirit of "damayan" in the Philippines, people in urban settings struggle to ask for or offer support during calamities, illness, or financial hardship due to a lack of community connection.
  • My Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Project Duration: Approximately 2–3 months
  • Tools Used: Figma

Research & Discovery

To ensure the design was rooted in cultural reality and responsive to community needs, research was conducted on how "damayan" manifests in Filipino communities and what users actually need.

  • User Research: Researched how "damayan" appears in Filipino communities — such as barangay systems, parokyano networks, and local lending groups — to ground the design in cultural context. Also conducted interviews with community members to understand their needs in asking for and offering help.
  • User Personas: Developed personas representing different community members — such as "Someone in Need of Help" and "Someone Offering Help" — to understand their motivations and pain points.
  • Competitive Analysis: Reviewed existing community apps or platforms that provide assistance to identify best practices and opportunities for improvement.

Ideation & Strategy

The strategy for the Damayan app focused on creating a hyper-local platform that is easy to use and accessible to all community members, with a clear information structure and user flow.

  • User Journey Maps: Mapped out user journeys for key tasks such as posting a request, browsing offers, and sending a message — to ensure a smooth and intuitive experience.
  • Information Architecture (IA): Outlined the app's information structure, including help categories, community profiles, and messaging system — to ensure easy discovery and access to information.
  • Wireframes (Low-Fidelity): Created low-fidelity wireframes for the app's key screens to quickly visualize layout and information flow, enabling early feedback collection.

Design & Execution

The Damayan app design was built with the goal of conveying community trust and warmth, using colors and icons that are easy to understand and accessible.

  • Design System: Designed screens with warm earth tones and illustrated icons to convey trust and community warmth. Prioritized large text, high-contrast colors, and simple navigation for accessibility — building a comprehensive design system.
  • High-Fidelity Designs: Built a complete mobile prototype in Figma with clear and accessible layouts, showcasing the final look of the screens with complete visual design.
  • Prototypes: Created an interactive Figma prototype of the full app to simulate the user experience and present the system flow to stakeholders and for usability testing.

Testing & Iteration

Testing and iteration were essential to ensure the design was effective and responsive to user needs — especially for a community app that demands high usability and accessibility.

  • Usability Testing: Conducted usability testing with target users, including those who are less tech-savvy, to identify any issues with usage, navigation, and feature comprehension.
  • Feedback & Changes: Based on feedback from usability testing, design changes were made to improve usability, accessibility, and the overall user experience.

Results & Reflection

The Damayan app aims to provide an easy-to-use platform for mutual aid — strengthening community solidarity and providing immediate assistance to those in need.

  • Impact / Metrics: Designed a hyper-local community app that makes it easy to post help requests, browse nearby offers, and connect with willing neighbors — usable even by less tech-savvy community members — expected to increase community engagement and speed up response to needs.
  • Key Takeaways: The project emphasized the importance of cultural research and accessibility-first design when building apps for diverse user bases, especially in sensitive contexts like mutual aid.
  • Next Steps: Potential addition of features such as emergency contact integration, expanded help categories, or partnerships with local NGOs and government agencies.
