Bottle

A conceptual UI design for a mobile water intake tracking app — designed to encourage users to stay hydrated through an intuitive and engaging interface. Built to promote healthier daily habits through simple, motivating interactions.

Gallery

Overview

Bottle is a conceptual UI design project for a mobile application focused on water intake tracking. The goal was to encourage users to stay hydrated through an intuitive and engaging interface — promoting a healthier lifestyle through simple, positive reinforcement.

  • Project Title: Bottle (Water Intake Tracker)
  • The Challenge: Many people struggle to drink enough water daily due to a lack of awareness or simply forgetting. There was a need for a simple and effective way to track water intake and send reminders to help users hit their daily hydration goals.
  • My Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Project Duration: Approximately 1–2 months
  • Tools Used: Figma

Research & Discovery

Research was conducted on users' water drinking habits and the factors that affect their hydration — to build an app that effectively helps them improve their daily intake.

  • User Research: Conducted interviews and surveys with potential users to understand their water drinking habits, pain points in tracking hydration, and the features they would want in a hydration tracker app.
  • User Personas: Developed personas such as the "Busy Professional" who needs reminders, and the "Health Enthusiast" who wants to track their progress — to guide design decisions.
  • Competitive Analysis: Reviewed existing hydration tracker apps to identify best practices, available features, and opportunities to differentiate the Bottle app.

Ideation & Strategy

The strategy focused on designing a simple and engaging user flow that makes tracking water intake effortless and provides positive reinforcement — with a clear information structure throughout.

  • User Journey Maps: Mapped the user journey from setting a daily goal, logging water intake, to viewing progress — prioritizing simplicity and quick interactions to ensure a smooth and intuitive experience.
  • Information Architecture (IA): Defined the app structure with sections for Daily Tracker, History, Goals, and Settings — ensuring easy navigation and access to all features.
  • Wireframes (Low-Fidelity): Created low-fi wireframes for all key screens to quickly visualize layout and information flow — enabling early feedback on structure and functionality.

Design & Execution

The Bottle app was designed with a clean and engaging aesthetic — using visual cues that encourage users to stay hydrated, backed by a complete visual design and interactive prototype.

  • Design System: Established a clean and engaging aesthetic with water-inspired colors (blues and whites) and playful illustrations. Used readable typography and clear iconography. Built a comprehensive component library — buttons, progress indicators, and notification designs — for visual consistency.
  • High-Fidelity Designs: Designed all final screens in Figma with complete visual design — including detailed UI elements, imagery, and interactive components that showcase the overall look and feel of the app.
  • Prototypes: Built a clickable Figma prototype to simulate the interactive user experience and present the flow to stakeholders for review and usability testing.

Testing & Iteration

Testing and iteration were conducted to ensure the design was effective and met user needs — identifying any usability issues or gaps in the reminder and tracking experience.

  • Usability Testing: Conducted usability testing with target users to identify issues with navigation, feature comprehension, and the overall hydration tracking experience.
  • Feedback & Changes: Based on testing feedback, design revisions were made to improve usability, visual appeal, and the overall user experience — resulting in a more refined and effective hydration tracker app.

Results & Reflection

The Bottle app aims to provide an easy-to-use platform for tracking daily water intake — encouraging users to stay hydrated and promoting a healthier lifestyle through consistent, positive habits.

  • Impact / Metrics: The designed app provides a simple and engaging way to track water intake, set daily goals, and receive reminders — expected to increase hydration levels and improve users' overall health and wellbeing.
  • Key Takeaways: The project reinforced the importance of creating an app that is not only functional but also motivating — and its direct impact on encouraging positive behavioral change in users.
  • Next Steps: Potential additions include personalized recommendations based on activity level and climate, integration with fitness trackers, and social sharing options to build community motivation.
