Alphatera Construction Supply

Figma B2B Platform UI/UX Web Design

A bold, industrial-themed UI/UX design for Alphatera Construction Supply — a B2B platform for construction materials. Designed in Figma to communicate strength, reliability, and professionalism through dark tones and structured layouts.

Gallery

Overview

The Alphatera Construction Supply project focused on creating a digital platform for a construction materials company. The primary goal was to establish an online presence and improve the sales process for B2B clients, providing a solid and professional image.

  • Project Title: Alphatera Construction Supply
  • The Challenge: Alphatera had no digital presence, making it difficult for B2B clients to find their services online and resulting in a fully manual sales process. They needed a platform to showcase their materials, services, and project portfolio while building credibility with buyers.
  • My Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Project Duration: Approximately 2–3 months
  • Tools Used: Figma

Research & Discovery

To build an effective B2B platform, understanding the needs of clients and the construction industry landscape was essential.

  • User Research: Conducted interviews with Alphatera stakeholders to understand their business goals, target B2B clients, and the key services and products they needed to showcase.
  • User Personas: Developed personas representing different types of B2B clients — such as Contractor, Procurement Manager, and Project Engineer — to understand their needs and purchasing processes.
  • Competitive Analysis: Reviewed competitor websites in the construction supply industry to identify best practices, market gaps, and opportunities for differentiation.

Ideation & Strategy

The website structure and user flow were outlined to ensure B2B clients could easily find information and get in touch with Alphatera — emphasizing simplicity and efficiency.

  • User Journey Maps: Mapped out user journeys for key tasks such as searching for products, viewing the project portfolio, and contacting sales — to ensure a smooth experience.
  • Information Architecture (IA): Outlined all pages and user flows: public site (Home, Services, Projects, Team, Contact) and admin dashboard flows, with logical categorization of information.
  • Wireframes (Low-Fidelity): Created low-fi wireframes for all pages to validate layout, content hierarchy, and navigation before applying visual design.

Design & Execution

The Alphatera design aimed to deliver a solid and professional image that reflects the construction industry, using an appropriate visual identity with clear call-to-actions.

  • Design System: Defined the visual identity with a dark navy and rust/orange palette inspired by construction materials and industrial environments. Used bold typography, construction-themed imagery, and a clear grid system for consistency.
  • High-Fidelity Designs: Produced polished screens with the full brand system applied. Used heavy hero images, structured grids, and clear CTAs throughout to guide users toward key actions.
  • Prototypes: Built an interactive Figma prototype to present the website flow and user experience to stakeholders and for initial testing.

Testing & Iteration

Testing and iteration were essential to ensure the design was effective and responsive to user needs.

  • Usability Testing: Conducted usability testing with target users to identify any issues with navigation, finding information, and overall user experience.
  • Feedback & Changes: Based on feedback from usability testing, design changes were made to improve usability and the overall effectiveness of the platform.

Results & Reflection

The project resulted in a website that communicates trust and capability — prioritizing fast access to services and project portfolio, enhancing Alphatera's digital presence.

  • Impact / Metrics: Designed a dark, industrial-themed B2B website that communicates trust and capability — prioritizing quick access to services, project showcase, and contact — expected to increase online visibility and leads.
  • Key Takeaways: The project highlighted the importance of building a strong brand identity through design and its impact on establishing credibility in the B2B market.
  • Next Steps: Potential addition of e-commerce functionality for direct materials purchasing, or development of a mobile app for on-site workers.
