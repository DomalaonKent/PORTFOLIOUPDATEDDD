Overview

The Tracking System is a UI design project for an operations tracking platform focused on real-time status monitoring, progress visibility, and team workflow management. The goal was to reduce operational blind spots and improve coordination between teams — particularly in managing vehicle trips and task assignments.

  • Project Title: Tracking System
  • The Challenge: Operations teams relied on manual status updates — spreadsheets, messages, and verbal reports — making it difficult to get an accurate real-time picture of ongoing tasks. This led to miscommunication, delays, and missed deadlines. Vehicle trip tracking in particular required a more structured and reliable system.
  • My Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Project Duration: Approximately 2–3 months
  • Tools Used: Figma

Research & Discovery

At this stage, core tracking needs were identified through user scenario analysis — understanding what teams need to see immediately versus on demand, especially for vehicle and trip monitoring.

  • User Research: Conducted interviews with operations managers, drivers, and dispatch personnel to understand their workflows, pain points with existing tracking methods, and requirements for an effective solution.
  • User Personas: Developed personas such as the "Operations Manager" who needs an overview of all trips, and the "Driver" who needs to update trip statuses — to guide design decisions.
  • Competitive Analysis: Reviewed existing fleet management and operations tracking systems to identify best practices, essential features, and opportunities for improvement.

Ideation & Strategy

The strategy centered on defining user flows that would simplify task creation, status updates, and team workload tracking — with a clear information structure for trips and vehicles.

  • User Journey Maps: Mapped out key user flows: creating a trip ticket, updating trip status, filtering trip views, and reviewing team workloads — to ensure a smooth and intuitive experience.
  • Information Architecture (IA): Defined the system structure with sections for Dashboard, Trip Tickets, Vehicles, and Profiles — ensuring easy access and navigation throughout.
  • Wireframes (Low-Fidelity): Created low-fi wireframes for key screens such as the login, trip ticket form, and dashboard to quickly visualize layout and information flow.

Design & Execution

The tracking system interface was built with a structured component system and clear information hierarchy — providing a centralized dashboard for operations monitoring with a complete visual design and interactive prototype.

  • Design System: Built the full interface with a structured component system, consistent status colors (e.g. green for completed, orange for draft), and clear information hierarchy — including defined typography, color palette, and iconography.
  • High-Fidelity Designs: Designed a centralized tracking dashboard where teams can monitor task status, assign owners, and visualize progress across departments. Screens include login, trip ticket form, dashboard, profile, vehicles, and trip tickets.
  • Prototypes: Built an interactive Figma prototype of the full system to simulate the user experience and present the flow to stakeholders for review and usability testing.

Testing & Iteration

Testing and iteration were essential to ensure the design was effective and responsive to user needs — especially for an operations tracking system that demands high accuracy and efficiency.

  • Usability Testing: Conducted usability testing with target users (operations managers, drivers) to identify issues with navigation, feature comprehension, and overall user experience.
  • Feedback & Changes: Based on testing feedback, design revisions were made to improve usability, visual clarity, and overall user experience — resulting in a more refined and effective system.

Results & Reflection

The tracking system aims to improve coordination and reduce operational blind spots by making it easy to spot bottlenecks at a glance — increasing team efficiency and productivity.

  • Impact / Metrics: Status indicators, filters, and timeline views make it easy to identify bottlenecks immediately — reducing operational blind spots and improving coordination. Expected outcomes include faster trip completion and more effective fleet management.
  • Key Takeaways: The project highlighted the importance of building a centralized, visually intuitive platform for operations tracking — and its direct impact on improving team communication and productivity.
  • Next Steps: Potential additions include GPS tracking integration, automated reporting, and mobile push notifications for real-time trip and driver updates.
