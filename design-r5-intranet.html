R5 V2 Intranet

Figma Enterprise Dashboard UI/UX Dark UI

A comprehensive enterprise intranet redesign for NTC Region V. The platform helps staff access regulatory data, documents, and telecom infrastructure monitoring through a clean, dark-themed interface optimized for long working hours and quick information scanning.

Gallery

Overview

The R5 V2 Intranet System project is a comprehensive redesign of the intranet for NTC Region V. It aims to resolve issues with the old system that made it difficult for staff to access important information and improve overall operations.

  • Project Title: R5 V2 Intranet System
  • The Challenge: NTC Region V was using an outdated intranet system with poor navigation, inconsistent UI, and no mobile support — slowing down daily operations and hindering access to regulatory data, document filing, and telecom infrastructure monitoring.
  • My Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Project Duration: Approximately 3–4 months
  • Tools Used: Figma

Research & Discovery

Thorough research was conducted to understand the specific needs of NTC Region V staff and the problems with the current system.

  • User Research: Conducted interviews with NTC staff to understand their pain points with the legacy system and their key workflows for regulatory compliance, document management, and data monitoring.
  • User Personas: Developed personas representing the different staff roles in NTC Region V — such as Regulatory Officer, IT Administrator, and Document Controller — to understand their needs and pain points.
  • Competitive Analysis: Reviewed existing intranet systems and enterprise solutions to identify best practices and opportunities for improvement.

Ideation & Strategy

Based on the research findings, the strategy and information architecture were outlined to create a more effective and user-friendly intranet system.

  • User Journey Maps: Mapped out user journeys for key tasks such as accessing regulatory data, uploading documents, and monitoring telecom infrastructure — to identify critical touchpoints and potential friction points.
  • Information Architecture (IA): Reorganized the navigation and layout to prioritize quick access to regulatory data, documents, and monitoring dashboards based on user roles.
  • Wireframes (Low-Fidelity): Created low-fidelity wireframes to quickly visualize page structure and information flow, enabling early feedback on layout and functionality.

Design & Execution

Ideas and strategy were translated into actual design, focused on creating a clear, efficient, and visually appealing interface with a consistent visual identity.

  • Design System: Built a dark theme to reduce eye strain and established a component system for dashboards, data tables, and KPI cards with clear visual hierarchy. Also defined typography, color palette, and iconography.
  • High-Fidelity Designs: Redesigned the platform from scratch in Figma with a focus on clarity and efficiency. Introduced a collapsible sidebar, role-based dashboards, and a dark-themed visual system with complete visual design.
  • Prototypes: Created a clickable Figma prototype to simulate the interactive user experience and present the system flow to stakeholders.

Testing & Iteration

To ensure the effectiveness of the design, testing and user feedback were conducted to continuously improve the system and address user needs.

  • Usability Testing: Gathered feedback from key users through usability testing to refine the layout, navigation patterns, and data visualization — and to identify any usability issues.
  • Feedback & Changes: Based on feedback from usability testing, the layout, navigation patterns, and data visualization were refined to improve the overall user experience and address identified problems.

Results & Reflection

The redesigned intranet aims to improve productivity and user experience for NTC Region V, providing faster and easier access to information.

  • Impact / Metrics: The redesigned platform helps staff access regulatory data, documents, and telecom infrastructure monitoring through a clean, dark-themed interface optimized for long working hours and quick information scanning — resulting in higher efficiency and productivity.
  • Key Takeaways: The project emphasized the importance of user research in building enterprise solutions and the impact of good design on the day-to-day operations of staff.
  • Next Steps: Potential expansion of features, such as integration with other internal systems, or development of a mobile application for on-the-go access.
